Computer case

A computer case, also known as a computer chassis, tower, system unit, or cabinet, is the enclosure that contains most of the components of a personal computer (usually excluding the display, keyboard, and mouse). Cases are usually constructed from steel (often SECC—steel, electrogalvanized, cold-rolled, coil), aluminium and plastic. Other materials such as glass, wood, acrylic and even Lego bricks have appeared in home-built cases.


The front of the computer case is usually equipped with:

  • power and reset buttons. The AT form factor cases usually have a Turbo button, Some early ATX cases have a separate standby button;
  • LED indicators showing computer operation, power and storage status, and hard drive status;
  • sockets for headphones and a microphone;
  • connectors for some external devices with data transfer interfaces (USB, FireWire).

The main (available on almost all ATX-format motherboards) connectors on the back of the case are:

  • PS/2 connectors for keyboard (purple) and mouse (green). You can also find a universal connector, vertically separated by two colors. At the moment it is obsolete: there is a tendency to replace this connector with more modern USB, but on low- and middle-price boards this but this connector(s) can still be found on low-end and mid-price boards;
  • 3.5mm jacks (3 or 6) of the integrated sound card, of which the main:
    • line output (green);
    • line input (blue);
    • microphone input (pink);
  • 4 to 8 (previously 2) USB connectors, in pairs;
  • connector for connecting to the local network.

Motherboard

Motherboards are the hardware that is the backbone of any desktop computer. The capabilities of this type of hardware can vary greatly. Manufacturers produce motherboards (this is the name of the motherboard is widely used), designed to solve any problems. Very popular nowadays motherboards Form-factors Micro-ATX and Mini-ITX, on the basis of which it is possible to create a compact computer used both at home and in office. Full-size boards (Standard-ATX and E-ATX) Give you the best flexibility for your system layout. The choice of motherboards depends not only on the form factor, but also on other features. You will need to determine Which brand of processor (Intel or AMD) you prefer. For any motherboard, the maximum allowable amount of RAM is specified. Gaming enthusiasts will surely pay attention to the number of PCI-E x16 slots and multiprocessor support. There is also a built-in Wi-Fi adaptor so you don't have to use any third-party devices for wireless networking. devices to connect to wireless networks.
